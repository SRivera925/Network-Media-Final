Nintendo's Expansion and Innovation Era: 1990s & 2000s

In the 1990s, Nintendo solidified its legacy with revolutionary titles on the *Super Nintendo Entertainment System (SNES)*, like *Super Mario World* (1990), *The Legend of Zelda: A Link to the Past* (1991), and *Donkey Kong Country* (1994). The *Nintendo 64* introduced 3D gaming with landmark titles such as *Super Mario 64* (1996) and *The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time* (1998). The 2000s saw continued innovation with the *GameCube* and the motion-controlled *Wii*, featuring games like *Super Smash Bros. Melee* (2001), *The Legend of Zelda: Twilight Princess* (2006), and *Wii Sports* (2006), which appealed to casual and family gamers.
